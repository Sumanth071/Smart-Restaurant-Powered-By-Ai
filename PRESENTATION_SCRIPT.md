## Speaker 1: K SUMANTH - Team Lead

Good morning everyone.

We are here to present our final year project, `AI-Powered Smart Restaurant Management System`. This project is developed using the `MERN` stack, which includes `MongoDB`, `Express.js`, `React.js`, and `Node.js`.

Our project aims to digitalize and simplify restaurant operations by combining booking, ordering, menu management, staff handling, reports, and AI-based support into one smart software platform.

Before explaining the project, let me introduce our team.

I am `K SUMANTH`, and I worked as the `Team Lead`. My contribution was leading the project execution, coordinating the overall development flow, and working on both frontend and backend integration.

My teammate `M SANTHOSH` worked as the `Business Analyst`. He focused on requirement gathering, workflow planning, and ensuring the project matched real restaurant business needs.

My teammate `SHAIK RABBANI` worked as the `Project Lead`. He contributed to planning, review, quality checks, and overall coordination of the project delivery.

Now I will explain the main objective of our project.

The purpose of this system is to provide a centralized web-based platform where all restaurant activities can be managed efficiently. In many restaurants, tasks like managing orders, bookings, reservations, staff, and reports are done manually or through disconnected systems. Our software solves this problem by integrating everything into one application.

This system supports four different user roles.

The first role is `Super Admin`. This role has complete access to the platform and can manage all restaurants, users, reports, and analytics.

The second role is `Restaurant Admin`. This role handles branch-specific management like menu, tables, bookings, and staff.

The third role is `Staff`. Staff can manage operational tasks like reservations, orders, and customer service actions.

The fourth role is `Guest User`. Guests can browse restaurants, view menu items, book tables, place food orders, and access customer support.

Now I will explain the technologies used.

For frontend development, we used `React` and `Tailwind CSS` to create a responsive and modern UI.

For backend development, we used `Node.js` and `Express.js` to build APIs and business logic.

For the database, we used `MongoDB` for storing restaurants, users, bookings, orders, menu items, and reports.

For security, we implemented `JWT authentication` to ensure secure login and role-based access.

## Speaker 2: M SANTHOSH - Business Analyst

Thank you, K Sumanth.

I will explain the business flow and functional modules of our project.

Our system is designed based on the real operational structure of restaurants. We identified the main problems in restaurant management, such as manual booking systems, poor order tracking, disconnected staff workflows, and lack of real-time business insights. Based on these problems, we designed the following modules.

The first module is `Authentication`.
Each user logs in securely with email and password. After login, the system identifies the role and grants only the relevant access.

The second module is `Dashboard`.
The dashboard displays important business information such as total users, restaurants, orders, reservations, bookings, and menu items. Each role sees a customized dashboard view.

The third module is `Restaurant Management`.
This module allows creation and management of restaurant branches. Admins can maintain branch details such as location, contact information, ratings, and service profile.

The fourth module is `Menu Management`.
This module is used to create, update, and manage food items, categories, prices, descriptions, and availability.

The fifth module is `Table Booking`.
Guests can book tables online by selecting the branch, date, time, and guest count. These bookings are stored and managed by the restaurant.

The sixth module is `Food Ordering`.
Customers can view available dishes and place online orders through the system. Orders are saved in the database and can be tracked by admins and staff.

The seventh module is `Reservation Management`.
This module helps manage scheduled dining requests, area preferences, guest counts, and special notes.

The eighth module is `User Management`.
Admins can manage users based on role, such as creating staff accounts, assigning permissions, and maintaining role-based access.

The ninth module is `Reports and Analytics`.
This module helps management understand restaurant performance through data like bookings, orders, reservations, and operational summaries.

Now let me explain how the roles interact with the system.

A `Guest User` visits the platform, explores restaurant details, checks the menu, books a table, or places a food order.

A `Restaurant Admin` logs in and manages daily restaurant-level operations such as menu updates, reservation handling, staff management, and customer activities.

A `Staff User` supports booking, ordering, and reservation execution.

A `Super Admin` oversees the full platform, including multiple branches and user roles.

This role-based structure makes the software practical and realistic for real-world use.
